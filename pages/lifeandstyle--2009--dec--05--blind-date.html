Blind date

Jeannine, 27, TV production manager, meets Tom, 30, design studio manager

Sat 5 Dec 2009 00.10 GMT

Jeannine on Tom

First impressions? A dapper man, good handshake. My nana always said you can judge a man on his handshake.

What did you talk about? Japan, the British “stiff upper lip”, India, running, tax, pub quizzes, his three-day week, what treacle really is…

Any awkward moments? Not one… well, perhaps when I managed to wrangle the chocolate brownie over treacle tart. I did feel slightly guilty.

Good table manners? Why yes, he even put his napkin on his lap…

Best thing about him? His self-deprecating humour and eyebrow-scratching tick.

Did you go on somewhere? Nope... just an entertaining walk to the tube station.

Marks out of 10? He made me agree I’d say 9!

Would you meet again? Sure… we never did finish discussing tax.

Tom on Jeannine

First impressions? Very cool, very pretty, really nice. And tall.

What did you talk about? Books, our jobs, her travelling, all sorts, really.

Any awkward moments? None.

Good table manners? Yes.

Best thing about her? She enjoys laughing.

Did you go on somewhere? No.

Marks out of 10? 8.5 – great company, fun conversation and a really nice evening all round.

Would you meet again? Yeah.

Jeannine and Tom ate at the Adam & Eve, London W1.

Fancy a blind date? Email blind.date@theguardian.com

This article was amended on 24 January 2020 to remove some personal information.
